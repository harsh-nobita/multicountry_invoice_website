Overview: Tax System Types

Business taxes on goods and services broadly fall into three categories. Understanding which applies to your country is essential before issuing invoices.

TypeCountriesHow It Works
VAT / GSTMost of the worldApplied at each production stage; businesses reclaim input tax.
Sales TaxUSA, some developing nationsApplied only at the final point of sale; no input tax credits.
No TaxHong Kong, Qatar, Kuwait, BVINo consumption tax on goods/services.

🇮🇳 GST – India

India's Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a comprehensive, multi-stage tax introduced on 1 July 2017. It replaced over a dozen central and state taxes.

Tax Rates

ExportsZero-rated0% (with refund)

Registration Requirements

  • Mandatory if annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh for North-Eastern states).
  • Service providers above ₹20 lakh must register regardless of state.
  • E-commerce sellers must register regardless of turnover.

Invoice Requirements

A GST-compliant tax invoice must include: supplier's GSTIN, recipient's GSTIN (for B2B), HSN/SAC code, place of supply, taxable value, and separately stated CGST/SGST or IGST amounts.

🇦🇺 GST – Australia

Australia's GST is a flat 10% tax on most goods, services, and other items sold or consumed in Australia.

RateApplies To
10%Standard goods and services
0%Fresh food, exports, health, education

You must register for GST if your turnover is AUD $75,000/year or more (AUD $150,000 for non-profit organisations). Your ABN (Australian Business Number) must appear on all tax invoices over AUD $82.50.

🇸🇬🇳🇿 GST – Singapore & New Zealand

Singapore GST

Singapore GST is currently 9% (raised from 8% on 1 January 2024). Registration is required when annual taxable turnover exceeds SGD $1 million.

New Zealand GST

New Zealand charges a flat 15% GST on all goods and services. Registration threshold is NZD $60,000/year. GST is typically included in the displayed price (GST-inclusive pricing).

🇪🇺 VAT – European Union

EU VAT is harmonized but rates vary by country. The EU minimum standard rate is 15%, but most countries charge 20–25%.

🇬🇧 VAT – United Kingdom

UK VAT rates: 20% standard, 5% reduced (home energy, children's car seats), 0% zero-rated (food, children's clothing, books).

Registration threshold: £90,000 taxable turnover in a 12-month period. VAT invoices must include your VAT registration number, tax point date, VAT rate, and VAT amount for each line.

🇮🇹🇪🇸🇲🇽 IVA – Italy, Spain & Mexico

Italy

Rates: 22% standard, 10% reduced (food, some services), 4% super-reduced (essential food, books).

Spain

Rates: 21% standard, 10% reduced (food, transport, hospitality), 4% super-reduced (basic food, medicine, books).

Mexico

Rates: 16% standard, 8% in border zones. Exports and some food/medicine items are 0%.

🇺🇸 Sales Tax – United States

The US has no federal VAT or GST. Sales tax is applied only at point of sale and is determined by state and sometimes local jurisdiction.

⚠️ Note: 5 states have no sales tax: Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, and Oregon.

🇲🇾 SST – Malaysia

Malaysia replaced GST with Sales and Service Tax (SST) in 2018. Service Tax is 6% on taxable services. Sales Tax applies at 5% or 10% on manufactured and imported goods.

🇮🇩 PPN – Indonesia

Indonesia's Pajak Pertambahan Nilai (PPN) is charged at 11% (raised from 10% in April 2022). Registration is required for businesses exceeding IDR 4.8 billion annually.

🇯🇵 JCT – Japan (消費税)

Japan's Consumption Tax is 10% for most goods and services, and 8% for food and non-alcoholic beverages (reduced rate). From October 2023, Japan adopted a qualified invoice system (適格請求書) requiring a registration number (T + 13 digits) on all tax invoices.
